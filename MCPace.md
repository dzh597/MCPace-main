
\section{Preliminaries} \subsection{Task Definition} A multimodal knowledge graph is denoted as \begin{equation} \mathcal{G}=(\mathcal{E},\mathcal{R},\mathcal{T},\mathcal{M}), \end{equation} where $\mathcal{E}$, $\mathcal{R}$ and $\mathcal{T}$ are the entity, relation and observed triple sets, and $\mathcal{M}$ denotes external modalities such as text and images. For entity $e$ and modality $m$, its raw modal information is denoted as $X_m(e)$. Following existing MMKGC settings, we treat graph structure as an intrinsic modality $S$, so each entity is represented by modality-specific embeddings: \begin{equation} \{\mathbf{e}_S,\mathbf{e}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{e}_M\}. \end{equation} The goal of MMKGC is to learn a scoring function \begin{equation} F:\mathcal{E}\times\mathcal{R}\times\mathcal{E}\rightarrow\mathbb{R}, \end{equation} which measures the plausibility of triples $(h,r,t)$ and ranks candidate entities for link prediction. \subsection{Modal Encoding and Fusion} For each external modality, raw features are encoded by a pre-trained encoder $PE_m(\cdot)$ and aggregated into an entity-level representation: \begin{equation} \mathbf{f}_m(e)=\frac{1}{|X_m(e)|}\sum_{x\in X_m(e)}PE_m(x), \end{equation} which is then projected into a unified embedding space: \begin{equation} \mathbf{e}_m=P_m(\mathbf{f}_m(e)). \end{equation} The structural embedding $\mathbf{e}_S$ is directly learned from triples. Since different relations may rely on different modalities, we adopt relation-aware adaptive fusion. For head entity $h$ under relation $r$, the modal weight is computed as \begin{equation} \omega_m(h,r)= \frac{ \exp(\mathbf{V}\odot\tanh(\mathbf{h}_m)/\sigma(\zeta_r)) }{ \sum_{n\in\mathcal{M}\cup\{S\}} \exp(\mathbf{V}\odot\tanh(\mathbf{h}_n)/\sigma(\zeta_r)) }, \end{equation} where $\mathbf{V}$ is learnable and $\zeta_r$ is a relation-aware temperature factor. The fused head representation is \begin{equation} \mathbf{h}_{\mathrm{joint}}= \sum_{m\in\mathcal{M}\cup\{S\}} \omega_m(h,r)\mathbf{h}_m, \end{equation} and $\mathbf{t}_{\mathrm{joint}}$ is defined analogously. We use a RotatE-style scoring function: \begin{equation} f(h,r,t)=- \|\mathbf{h}_{\mathrm{joint}}\circ\mathbf{r} -\mathbf{t}_{\mathrm{joint}}\|, \end{equation} where $\circ$ denotes rotation in complex space. The model is optimized with a negative-sampling objective: \begin{equation} \begin{aligned} \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{kgc}} =& \sum_{(h,r,t)\in\mathcal{T}} -\log\sigma(\gamma+f(h,r,t)) \\ &- \sum_{i=1}^{K} p_i\log\sigma(-f(h_i',r_i',t_i')-\gamma), \end{aligned} \end{equation} where $\gamma$ is the margin, $K$ is the number of negative samples, and $p_i$ is the self-adversarial negative sampling weight. \subsection{Modal Gradients} Although relation-aware fusion adjusts modal importance at the representation level, it cannot directly control modal optimization during backpropagation. Therefore, we define the gradient contribution of modality $m$ as \begin{equation} \mathbf{g}_h^m = \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial\mathbf{h}_{\mathrm{joint}}} \cdot \frac{\partial\mathbf{h}_{\mathrm{joint}}}{\partial\mathbf{h}_m}, \end{equation} \begin{equation} \mathbf{g}_t^m = \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial\mathbf{t}_{\mathrm{joint}}} \cdot \frac{\partial\mathbf{t}_{\mathrm{joint}}}{\partial\mathbf{t}_m}. \end{equation} The complete modal gradient is \begin{equation} \mathbf{g}_m=[\mathbf{g}_h^m;\mathbf{g}_t^m]. \end{equation} This allows us to measure modal contribution by its actual effect on optimization rather than by static modal weights. \subsection{Relation-aware Accumulated Contribution Energy} To estimate long-term modal participation, we define Relation-aware Accumulated Contribution Energy (RACE). For modality $m$ at step $T$, its global accumulated energy is \begin{equation} E_m^{(T)} = \sum_{t=1}^{T} \|\eta_t\mathbf{g}_m^{(t)}\|_2^2, \end{equation} where $\eta_t$ is the effective learning rate. To capture relation-specific modal preference, we further define \begin{equation} E_{m,r}^{(T)} = \sum_{t=1}^{T} \mathbb{I}[r_t=r] \cdot \|\eta_t\mathbf{g}_m^{(t)}\|_2^2. \end{equation} RACE identifies whether a modality is continuously involved or gradually marginalized under each relation. 

\subsection{Modality Coordination Plane} To jointly describe modal imbalance and gradient conflict, we introduce the Modality Coordination Plane (MCP). For a modality pair $(i,j)$, their relative contribution is \begin{equation} w_i=\frac{E_i}{E_i+E_j+\epsilon}, \quad w_j=1-w_i, \end{equation} and the balance coefficient is \begin{equation} \bar{w}_{ij}=8w_iw_j-1. \end{equation} Here, $\bar{w}_{ij}=1$ indicates balanced contribution, while $\bar{w}_{ij}=-1$ indicates extreme domination by one modality. Gradient direction consistency is measured by \begin{equation} c_{ij} = \frac{\mathbf{g}_i^\top\mathbf{g}_j} {\|\mathbf{g}_i\|_2\|\mathbf{g}_j\|_2+\epsilon}. \end{equation} A positive $c_{ij}$ indicates cooperative gradients, while a negative value indicates conflict. Thus, each modality pair is mapped to \begin{equation} (\bar{w}_{ij},c_{ij})\in[-1,1]\times[-1,1], \end{equation} where MCP provides a compact description of contribution balance and gradient consistency.



\section{Methodology} Based on modal gradients, RACE and MCP, we propose \textbf{MCPace}. The overall workflow of the proposed MCPace framework is illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:workflow}. MCPace coordinates modalities in gradient space: aligned modalities are enhanced, conflicting modalities are decoupled with minimal perturbation, and long-term contribution imbalance is compensated through energy-aware rebalancing. \subsection{Energy-aware Rebalancing} For each modality $i$, we first compute a base rebalancing coefficient from RACE:
\begin{equation} r_i=\frac{\sum_{m=1}^{M}E_m}{M\cdot (E_i+\epsilon)}, \end{equation} 
where $M$ is the number of modalities and $\epsilon$ is a small constant for numerical stability. A smaller accumulated energy $E_i$ yields a larger $r_i$, thereby compensating under-optimized modalities. Since modality $i$ may interact with multiple other modalities, we aggregate pairwise gradient consistency before applying rebalancing. For modality pair $(i,j)$, let
\begin{equation} c_{ij}= \frac{\mathbf{g}_i^\top \mathbf{g}_j} {\|\mathbf{g}_i\|_2\|\mathbf{g}_j\|_2+\epsilon} \end{equation}
denote their cosine similarity. We define the interaction weight between modality $i$ and modality $j$ as 
\begin{equation} \rho_{ij}= \frac{|c_{ij}|+\epsilon} {\sum_{l\neq i}(|c_{il}|+\epsilon)}. \end{equation} 
Then the aggregated consistency score of modality $i$ is 
\begin{equation} \bar{c}_i=\sum_{j\neq i}\rho_{ij}c_{ij}. \end{equation} 
The final conflict-aware rebalancing coefficient is defined as 
\begin{equation} \tilde{r}_i=r_i^{\frac{1+\bar{c}_i}{2}}. \end{equation} 
When modality $i$ is mostly aligned with other modalities, $\bar{c}_i$ is large and the compensation is stronger. When modality $i$ has strong conflicts with other modalities, $\bar{c}_i$ becomes smaller and the rebalancing effect is suppressed, preventing weak modalities from being blindly amplified under destructive interference.

\subsection{Cooperative Enhanced Projection} When $c_{ij}\geq0$, modalities $i$ and $j$ are directionally consistent. To enhance their cooperation while preserving local modal structure, each modal gradient is divided into $L$ blocks: 
\begin{equation} \mathbf{g}_i=[\mathbf{g}_i^{(1)},\ldots,\mathbf{g}_i^{(L)}]. \end{equation} 
For the $k$-th block, we construct a projected gradient: 
\begin{equation} \mathbf{P}_i^{(k)}=\alpha_i^{(k)}\mathbf{g}_i^{(k)}. \end{equation} 
The coefficient $\alpha_i^{(k)}$ is obtained by minimizing a local Dirichlet energy. For modality pair $(i,j)$ and block $k$, we define 
\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} \mathcal{D}_{ij}^{(k)} = \frac{a_{ij}^{(k)}}{2} \left( \alpha_i^{(k)}\|\mathbf{g}_i^{(k)}\|_2 - \alpha_j^{(k)}\|\mathbf{g}_j^{(k)}\|_2 \right)^2 \\+ \frac{\lambda_{\alpha}}{2} \left[ (\alpha_i^{(k)}-1)^2 + (\alpha_j^{(k)}-1)^2 \right], \end{aligned} \end{equation} 
where
\begin{equation} a_{ij}^{(k)}=\max(c_{ij}^{(k)},0),\end{equation}  \begin{equation}\quad c_{ij}^{(k)}= \frac{(\mathbf{g}_i^{(k)})^\top \mathbf{g}_j^{(k)}} {\|\mathbf{g}_i^{(k)}\|_2\|\mathbf{g}_j^{(k)}\|_2+\epsilon}. \end{equation} 
The first term encourages locally aligned modalities to have smooth block-level gradient strengths, while the second term prevents excessive deviation from the original gradient. This optimization is a convex quadratic problem. Let $x_i^{(k)}=\|\mathbf{g}_i^{(k)}\|_2$ and $x_j^{(k)}=\|\mathbf{g}_j^{(k)}\|_2$. The optimal coefficients are obtained by solving 

\begin{equation}\small \begin{bmatrix} a_{ij}^{(k)}(x_i^{(k)})^2+\lambda_{\alpha} & -a_{ij}^{(k)}x_i^{(k)}x_j^{(k)} \\ -a_{ij}^{(k)}x_i^{(k)}x_j^{(k)} & a_{ij}^{(k)}(x_j^{(k)})^2+\lambda_{\alpha} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \alpha_i^{(k)} \\ \alpha_j^{(k)} \end{bmatrix} = \lambda_{\alpha} \begin{bmatrix} 1\\ 1 \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation} 
Thus, the local projection has a closed-form linear-system solution and can be efficiently computed for each modality pair and gradient block.
\subsection{Conflict Orthogonal Projection} When $c_{ij}<0$, modalities $i$ and $j$ have conflicting optimization directions. We introduce perturbations $\mathbf{O}_{i\leftarrow j}$ and $\mathbf{O}_{j\leftarrow i}$ to reduce their conflict with minimal modification. Instead of solving the original nonlinear orthogonality constraint directly, we use a first-order linearized constraint:
\begin{equation} \mathbf{g}_i^\top\mathbf{g}_j + \mathbf{O}_{i\leftarrow j}^\top\mathbf{g}_j + \mathbf{g}_i^\top\mathbf{O}_{j\leftarrow i} =0. \end{equation} 
The perturbations are obtained by solving 
\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} \min_{\mathbf{O}_{i\leftarrow j},\mathbf{O}_{j\leftarrow i}} & \quad w_i\|\mathbf{O}_{i\leftarrow j}\|_2^2 + w_j\|\mathbf{O}_{j\leftarrow i}\|_2^2, \\ \mathrm{s.t.} & \quad \mathbf{g}_i^\top\mathbf{g}_j + \mathbf{O}_{i\leftarrow j}^\top\mathbf{g}_j + \mathbf{g}_i^\top\mathbf{O}_{j\leftarrow i} =0, \end{aligned} \end{equation} 
where $ w_i=\frac{E_i}{E_i+E_j+\epsilon}, \quad w_j=\frac{E_j}{E_i+E_j+\epsilon}. $


Let $s_{ij}=\mathbf{g}_i^\top\mathbf{g}_j<0$ and  $ D_{ij}= \frac{\|\mathbf{g}_j\|_2^2}{w_i+\epsilon} + \frac{\|\mathbf{g}_i\|_2^2}{w_j+\epsilon} +\epsilon.$
The closed-form solution is 
\begin{equation} \mathbf{O}_{i\leftarrow j} = -\frac{s_{ij}}{D_{ij}} \cdot \frac{\mathbf{g}_j}{w_i+\epsilon}, \quad \mathbf{O}_{j\leftarrow i} = -\frac{s_{ij}}{D_{ij}} \cdot \frac{\mathbf{g}_i}{w_j+\epsilon}. \end{equation} 
This operation suppresses first-order gradient conflict with minimal perturbation. Modalities with larger accumulated contribution are perturbed less, preserving stable dominant gradients. 
\subsection{Consensus-based Coordinated Updates} 
Sequential pairwise corrections may be inconsistent when multiple modalities are involved. To avoid order-dependent updates, MCPace computes all pairwise corrections from the original modal gradients in parallel and aggregates them through a consensus update. For each modality pair $(i,j)$, we first construct a candidate gradient $\mathbf{z}_{i\leftarrow j}$. If $c_{ij}\geq0$, cooperative enhancement is applied:
\begin{equation}  \small \mathbf{z}_{i\leftarrow j} = \tilde{r}_i \cdot \mathrm{Concat}_{k=1}^{L} \left[ \gamma_l^{(k)}\mathbf{P}_i^{(k)} + (1-\gamma_l^{(k)})\mathbf{g}_i^{(k)} \right], \end{equation} 
where $ \gamma_l^{(k)}=\sigma(q_{ij}c_{ij}^{(k)}). $ If $c_{ij}<0$, we use conflict decoupling: 
\begin{equation} \mathbf{z}_{i\leftarrow j} = \tilde{r}_i\mathbf{g}_i + (1-\gamma_g)\mathbf{O}_{i\leftarrow j}, \end{equation} 
where $ \gamma_g=\sigma(q_{ij}c_{ij}), q_{ij}=4\bar{w}_{ij}.$ Finally, the modified gradient of modality $i$ is obtained by solving a consensus aggregation problem: 
\begin{equation}\begin{aligned}
 \mathbf{g}_i^{\mathrm{mod}} = \arg\min_{\mathbf{z}} \left\|\mathbf{z}-\tilde{r}_i\mathbf{g}_i\right\|_2^2  \\+ \mu\sum_{j\neq i}\rho_{ij} \left\|\mathbf{z}-\mathbf{z}_{i\leftarrow j}\right\|_2^2,\end{aligned} \end{equation} 
where $\mu$ controls the strength of pairwise coordination. This objective has the following closed-form solution: 
\begin{equation} \mathbf{g}_i^{\mathrm{mod}} = \frac{ \tilde{r}_i\mathbf{g}_i + \mu\sum_{j\neq i}\rho_{ij}\mathbf{z}_{i\leftarrow j} } {1+\mu}. \end{equation} 
This consensus update avoids order-dependent pairwise corrections and produces a unified modal gradient. It jointly accounts for accumulated contribution, local cooperation and global conflict, enabling MCPace to coordinate gradient magnitude and direction.
